How We Started

World Rhythm Culture was born from a simple observation: in a world increasingly disconnected, rhythm remains an undeniable unifying force.

What began as a small gathering of percussion enthusiasts in a local park has blossomed into a global initiative. We realized that when people drum together, barriers of language, status, and background dissolve instantly.

Today, we facilitate drum circles, workshops, and immersive rhythmic experiences designed to foster community, reduce stress, and ignite pure joy.

Our Mission

To make the transformative power of group drumming accessible to everyone, everywhere.

Our Vision

A world where rhythmic expression is recognized as a fundamental human need and a tool for global peace.

Core Philosophy

Inclusivity over technicality. The groove is greater than the sum of its parts.

Meet the Rhythm Keepers

The passionate team behind World Rhythm Culture, dedicated to bringing people together.

Madhu S Srinivas

Drummer & Facilitator

Madhu S Srinivas is a drummer, multi-percussionist, and drum circle facilitator who began his journey with classical Tabla. A self-taught artist on other percussion instruments, he plays more than 14 kinds of percussion instruments. He performs across diverse genres like classical fusion, rock and roll, electronic, and metal, having completed 3000+ shows across India.

Udaya Chandra Aradhya

Percussionist & Educator

Udaya Chandra Aradhya is a passionate percussion artist and educator who began his musical journey with the tabla at the age of four. With years of experience and dedication, he has mastered multiple percussion instruments, including the tabla, cajon, djembe, and darbuka. He is the founder of Nandi School of Passion, where he nurtures aspiring musicians and shares his love for rhythm and music. Alongside his artistic achievements, he holds a BBA and MBA, blending creativity with a strong academic foundation.

Karthik

Theatre Artist & Percussionist

Karthik is a Bangalore-based software engineer, theatre artist, and percussion enthusiast with a lifelong passion for the performing arts. Actively involved in Kannada theatre since 1994, he has performed in over 150 stage productions and contributed as a rhythmist in more than 25 theatre plays, enriching performances through live percussion and rhythm. He is closely associated with the theatre groups Sanchaya and Navodaya and has trained under veteran playwright and theatre personality G. B. Kote Ananth. Alongside his theatre journey, he developed a strong interest in music and received formal training in Keyboard from V. Nanjundaiah, which laid the foundation for his musical pursuits. Continuing to expand his artistic horizons, he is currently learning the Djembe under the guidance of Madhu S, exploring the rich world of rhythm and percussion. A dedicated percussionist and avid collector of musical instruments, Karthik enjoys exploring diverse rhythms, sounds, and cultural traditions. His unique blend of technology, theatre, and music reflects a deep commitment to creativity, storytelling, and artistic expression.
